# MOLT — itch.io page copy

## Tagline

You are the crab. Your carapace is the paddle. An arkanoidvania for Playdate.

## Description

MOLT is a breakout game shaped like a Metroidvania. You are a small crab
on the sea bed, bouncing a pearl off your own carapace to smash coral,
kelp, stone and shipwreck across 28 connected rooms in six zones. Where
the pearl lands on your shell sets its flight; the crank adds aim and
english; burrowing into the sand is both a dodge and a deliberate
let-it-pass play.

Seven guardians of the deep — a hermit in a bottle, a kelp horse, a
siren, Umibōzu, Charybdis, a jousting seahorse knight, and finally the
Kraken with tentacles that fight as rival paddles — each yield a molt:
a new shell with a new power. Pincer Snap cuts kelp, Sticky Claw catches
for aimed serves, Heavy Pearl shatters stone, the Lantern Snail lights
the Abyss, Anchor Legs defy the crush-currents. Gates stay broken
forever; three hidden temple keys open the endgame. All 1-bit art, all
synthesized audio, zero asset files.

## Features

- Arkanoid moment to moment, Metroid in structure: 28 rooms, 6 zones,
  one connected map with backtracking and permanent gates
- 7 boss fights, each with its own mechanic — ending in a 3-phase
  Kraken whose tentacles are rival paddles
- 5 molt abilities that visibly change your shell
- 8 field creatures that bend trajectories and contest the floor —
  they stun, never wound; only dropped pearls cost hearts
- Crank aiming, bounce english, and full docked-crank play
- Shell-shard heart upgrades, rest anemone saves, world map
- Clock-driven step-sequencer soundtrack: a motif per zone plus a
  driving boss variant, all synth
- 1-bit, 400×240, 30fps; original game

## Controls

d-pad ←/→ scuttle · ↓ burrow / descend · ↑ ride bubble vents ·
A serve / Pincer Snap · B hold catch, tap map ·
crank aims serves and bends bounces (docked crank fully supported)

## Install (no dev tools needed)

1. Download `Molt.pdx.zip` from the Releases page (or `dist/`).
2. Sideload it at <https://play.date/account/sideload/> — or unzip and
   open the `.pdx` in the Playdate Simulator from the free Playdate SDK.
