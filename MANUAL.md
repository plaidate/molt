# Molt — Player's Manual

*An arkanoidvania: you are the crab, your carapace is the paddle.*

## The story

A small crab wakes on the sea bed with nothing but a pearl and a shell.
The deep is sealed by kelp, stone and glyph — and guarded by seven myths,
from a hermit in a bottle to the Kraken itself. Every guardian you humble
lets you **molt**: shed your old shell, grow a stranger one, and reach
water no crab has scuttled before.

## Controls

| Input | Action |
| --- | --- |
| d-pad ← / → | scuttle along the sand (walk off an edge to leave the room) |
| d-pad ↑ | ride a bubble vent up through the ceiling |
| d-pad ↓ | burrow into the sand — dodge shots, or sink through a floor gap |
| A | serve the pearl (while held) / **Pincer Snap** (while it flies) |
| B (hold) | **Sticky Claw** catch — the pearl sticks to your claw |
| B (tap) | world map |
| Crank | aim the serve; crank *during* a bounce for up to 20° of english |
| Crank docked | fixed 45° serves toward mid-screen — fully playable docked |

The system menu also has a **world map** item and a **music** toggle.

## How to play

Bounce the pearl off your carapace to smash blocks. Where it lands on
your shell sets the exit angle — dead centre goes straight up, the rim
sends it flying sideways (up to 65° of tilt). Cranking as it touches
bends the shot further.

Rooms are single screens connected into one undersea world: walk off the
left or right edge, ride bubble vents up, burrow through sand gaps down.
Leaving a room pockets the pearl — serving again is always free, so
explore without babysitting the ball.

**Missing the pearl costs one shell heart.** At zero hearts you "molt
back": the tide carries you to the last rest anemone you touched, hearts
full, upgrades and map intact. Rest anemones (one in the Tidepools, one
in the Kelp Forest) heal you completely and mark your save; the game
also saves at every molt, gate, and key.

Field blocks regrow when you re-enter a room. Gates — kelp curtains,
stone seals, glyph blocks, key blocks — stay broken **forever**.

## The five molts

Each boss guards a shrine. Touch it after the fight to molt:

1. **Pincer Snap** (Tidepools) — A while the pearl flies: cuts kelp
   curtains near you and super-shots a low, falling pearl back up at
   1.35× speed. Also startles a moray into spitting your pearl out.
2. **Sticky Claw** (Kelp Forest) — hold B to catch the pearl on your
   claw, then crank a fresh aimed serve. The precision instrument.
3. **Heavy Pearl** (Coral Reef) — the pearl shatters stone and hull
   iron (two hits), at a touch slower pace.
4. **Lantern Snail** (Shipwreck) — a glowing snail rides your shell.
   Dark rooms light up around you *and* around the pearl in flight.
5. **Anchor Legs** (Abyss) — the crush-currents of the deep can no
   longer drag you; the last doors open.

## Pickups & progression

- **Shell shards** drop from marked coral and bosses' hoards; collect
  four to grow a new maximum heart (up to six). Growing refills you.
- **Temple keys** (3) sit in optional vault rooms off the beaten path —
  each needs a later molt to reach. All three loosen the glyph door to
  the Sunken Temple.
- **Boss wounds persist** while you play: retreat from a fight, heal,
  and return to find the guardian still hurt.

## Creatures of the field

Creatures stun and shove — they never cost hearts. Only a dropped pearl
does. Most die to one pearl hit and return when you re-enter the room.

| Creature | Habit |
| --- | --- |
| Jellyfish | drifts in slow loops; stuns you if it sinks to your lane |
| Pufferfish | inflates when the pearl comes near — a puffed puffer deflects it hard in a random direction; deflate-window hits kill it |
| Starfish | crawls the field regrowing broken coral — a repair crew worth squashing |
| Urchin | a spiked floor squatter that shrinks your lane; takes two hits; stuns unless you burrow past |
| Barnacle turret | spits floor-skimming pellets at you — burrow and let them pass |
| Moray eel | lunges from a porthole and swallows the pearl; it spits eventually, but a Pincer Snap by the porthole gets it back now |
| Ghost fish | an Abyss ram you only see in lantern light |
| Sprat shoal | a harmless silver cloud — free bounces that scatter it apart |

## The seven guardians (hints only)

- **The Old Hermit** (Tidepools) — a crab in bottle armor. No shell
  plate, no protection.
- **The Kelpie** (Kelp Forest) — gallops the ceiling and sows kelp in
  your lane. Keep your pincer busy.
- **The Siren** (Coral Reef) — her song bends your pearl toward her
  while the choir sings. Consider the choir.
- **Umibōzu** (Shipwreck) — the sea itself goes dark. Eyes that are
  open can be closed.
- **Charybdis** (Abyss) — swallows your pearl and spits it where it
  pleases. Something in this room disagrees with its stomach.
- **The Hippocampus** (Sunken Temple) — a seahorse knight who jousts
  your paddle lane. Never strike a charging shield.
- **THE KRAKEN** — its tentacles rise as rival paddles and bat your
  pearl away. The eye opens between waves. Three phases. Good luck,
  little crab.

## Tips

1. **Aim with your shell, not just the crank.** Position so the pearl
   lands off-centre toward your target — rim hits sweep almost sideways.
2. **English is free.** A quick crank flick as the pearl touches down
   bends the shot up to 20° without moving an inch.
3. **Burrowing is a parry.** Pellets, urchins, jousts and tentacle
   sweeps all miss a buried crab — and you can deliberately let the
   pearl pass overhead when a bounce would be worse than a catch-up.
4. **Hold B early.** Once you have the Sticky Claw, catching every
   return turns the whole game into aimed serves — keys, choir coral
   and boss eyes stop being luck.
5. **A snap is a rescue.** A pearl about to drop near you can be
   super-shot instead of bounced — faster, steeper, and it counts as a
   hit on whatever it meets.
6. **Losing is cheap.** Hearts refill at anemones and molts, gates stay
   broken, boss wounds keep. When a fight goes badly, walking out is a
   strategy, not a defeat.
